I Boosted Instagram Reach by 13x in 30 Days.

Increased a metric by 10 fold? That's a rookie number, yet still a positive indicator that I'm doing something right. Here's how I did it.

About The Instagram Account

Fig. 1: Pie chart representing the total number of posts made in 2024, classified by the type of post.

The first post ever made on @nadraqamaria was posted on January 7, 2024. It was an introduction to my office, AKA my bedroom, and a super brief summary of what The Nadra Agency (TNA) stood for. Back then, it was affordable web design without the focus on lead generation for small businesses, contrary to present day.

It was a solid post. By the end of 2024, it got in the list of top 3 best performing image posts of the year.

The goal then was to just put my name out there. A good start.

Problem Faced

During August 2024, I had just returned from Germany and my brain itched for a new client project. A way I got attention from prospects was through social media, so I took a look at how the Instagram was performing the month before, which was July.

TLDR; using reach as a measurement of success, it didn't look good.

Fig. 2: Line chart representing number of reach obtained by each post of every type, from Jan to Aug 2024.

The number was generally decreasing, especially because I only posted 6 times on that month of July, instead of the minimum 12. I think I was parading during Christopher's Day in Berlin around those dates ...

Research

As a personal principle of mine, consistency is important to achieve trackable growth. It's more effective than experimenting sporadically and recklessly. With that approach, how do you know which of your actions taken is counter productive? How do you know which exact item needs working on?

I learnt that lesson in the most time-consuming way ever. (Mental note: Content idea)

Therefore, time to make use of Meta Business Suite for analytics. I exported content analytics data from the first and second quarter of 2024 to find out what types of content I should continue posting, and what I should stop posting.

Let's dive deeper. When I organised the data by post type--image, carousel, and reel--you'll notice that one type of post stands out massively.

Fig. 3: Line chart representing number of reach obtained by each post from Jan to Aug 2024, grouped by post type.

Can you guess which type of post that is?

Out of the 5 best posts from January to July 2024, 4 of them are Instagram reels!

Fig. 4: A table representing the top 5 best performing posts of all types, from January to July 2024.

Hence, reels bring better results, and have great audience reach if they feature candid clips from your daily life.

Solution

Moving on, I proposed to myself a slight alteration on the current strategy--to spend less time on the "knowledge" and "nurture" content pillars to focus on the "authenticity" pillar.

The new main objective was no longer to educate on websites and lead generation (it became the secondary). Instead, I focused more on sharing lessons I learnt regarding business and personal development.

For each caption, I made sure to add my own opinions and how I personally implemented those learning points in my life.

Results

Welcome to 2025!

The end of the year came, and I was eager to learn how my Instagram did during the last quarter of 2024. I'll let the numbers speak for themselves:

Fig. 5: Line chart representing reach obtained by all types of posts in 2024. The red dashed line indicates the date 31 July, 2024.

The greatest number of reach achieved was 1,079 as of the end of 2024. It was an Instagram reel posted on 10 December 2024, which described my progress building a beginner portfolio for a tech career in data.

The lowest number of reach was 13, obtained by a carousel post shared on 11 December, 2024. It was a mini guide on how to "turn website visitors into paying customers".

The highest number of likes, 18, was obtained by a single image post about a business shift within The Nadra Agency (TNA) on 3 October, 2024. Frankly, it was more about an entrepreneurial crisis I was personally overcoming.

The least liked post? A carousel that gave away actionable suggestions to get your website in front of more visitors. In hindsight, the content was probably too jargon-y. Hm.

Fig. 7: Box plot representing the distribution of the reach metric across all post types.

Generally, the number of reach increased for the reels, from a median of 64.5 to 75. However, reach decreased for both images and carousels.

There seems to be more promise towards posting Instagram reels.

Fig. 8: Box plot representing the distribution of the likes metric across all post types

The number of likes across all post types generally decreased over time.

Conclusion

These insights are strange, but liberating.

Knowing that I don't have to talk about the agency in every post frees my mind to explore other topics. Other topics that are just as important to me.

  • In order to gain more reach, I will share more personal stories that allow my audience step into my shoes as a solopreneur and flawed human being. I will do less of the nerdy content that talks too much about technicalities startup founders shouldn't worry about.
  • In order to gain more engagement, expressing personal struggles in a vulnerable yet enlightening way is the key. Again, I'll skip the nerdy website and CRO content more.

I can't wait see the growth that will take place for The Nadra Agency (TNA) in 2025.
